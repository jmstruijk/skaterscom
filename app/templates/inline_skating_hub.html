What is Inline Skating?

Inline skating, also known as rollerblading, is a form of skating using boots with wheels arranged in a single line. Inline skating combines elements of ice skating and roller skating, offering a smooth, fast-paced experience on paved surfaces. It's popular for recreation, fitness, urban transportation, and competitive sports.

Inline skating venues include dedicated skate paths, bike trails, boardwalks, parks, and specialized facilities. Many cities offer miles of paved trails perfect for inline skating, while some facilities provide indoor spaces for year-round skating regardless of weather conditions.
