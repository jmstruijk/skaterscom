What is a Skate Park?

A skate park is a purpose-built recreational facility designed for skateboarding, BMX biking, scootering, and inline skating. Skate parks feature various obstacles and terrain including ramps, rails, ledges, stairs, bowls, and vert ramps that allow riders to perform tricks and practice their skills in a safe, controlled environment.

Modern skate parks range from simple concrete slabs with basic obstacles to elaborate facilities with multiple sections catering to different skill levels. They can be indoor or outdoor, free public parks or paid private facilities, and serve everyone from beginners learning basics to professional skaters perfecting advanced tricks.
